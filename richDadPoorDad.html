Genre Personal Finance, Self-Help, Business & Economics
Author Authored by Robert Kiyosaki and Sharon Lechter
Index Page Financial Literacy: Understanding the basics of finance and how money works.
Cash Flow Quadrant: A framework for categorizing individuals based on their income sources: Employees, Self-Employed, Business Owners, and Investors.
Assets vs. Liabilities: Differentiating between items that generate income (assets) and those that incur expenses (liabilities).
Mindset and Wealth: The importance of adopting a mindset that supports wealth creation and financial independence.
Overview "Rich Dad Poor Dad" is a personal finance book that contrasts the financial mindsets of two father figures: Kiyosaki's own "poor dad" and his best friend's "rich dad." It emphasizes financial literacy, financial independence, and building wealth through investing in assets like real estate and businesses. The book teaches readers to differentiate between assets and liabilities and encourages them to work towards financial freedom by making money work for them rather than working for money
